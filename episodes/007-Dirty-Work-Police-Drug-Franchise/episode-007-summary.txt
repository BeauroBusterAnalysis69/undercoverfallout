Episode 007: Shadows of Justice - A Deep Dive Into the Police State

SUMMARY:
A raw, unfiltered deep dive into the dark machinery of American policing. Starting with the El Paso paradox - why is one of America's safest cities just yards from one of the world's most violent? The answer leads to the "dirty work theory" of border enforcement, the blue wall of silence, and allegations of police operating as a drug franchise.

KEY TOPICS:
- The El Paso/Juarez paradox and "invisible violence"
- Dirty work theory: extrajudicial enforcement on the border
- The blue wall of silence and why "good cops" are a myth
- The drug cycling theory: seized drugs recycled back to the streets
- Case study: Mental health crisis met with violence
- Case study: A 17-year-old framed with a planted petri dish in Plano
- The 98% conviction rate and the plea deal machine
- Poverty traps: How a broken taillight leads to jail
- The extraction model: Mining the poor for fines and fees
- Disenfranchisement: Stripping voting rights from system victims
- The narco-terrorist police state theory
- China's fentanyl supply chain as chemical warfare
- "The war is in a room" - decisions that destroy lives
- AI surveillance: The coming automated police state

RUNTIME: ~37 minutes
